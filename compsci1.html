The time complexity of the Quicksort algorithm varies based on the scenario:

1. Best Case (O(n log n))

  • In the best-case scenario, the partition process divides the array into two equal halves.
  • This results in a recurrence relation: T(n) = 2 * T(n/2) + O(n).
  • The solution to this recurrence relation is O(n log n).

2. Average Case (O(n log n))

  • In the average case, the partitions are not exactly equal but are reasonably balanced.
  • The average-case complexity is also O(n log n), requiring more complex mathematical analysis.

3. Worst Case (O(n^2))

  • The worst-case scenario occurs when the partition process always picks the greatest or smallest element as the pivot.
  • This results in one of the sub-arrays always being empty, leading to T(n) = T(n-1) + O(n).
  • Solving this recurrence relation gives a complexity of O(n^2).

Note: The average case complexity is more representative of Quicksort's real-world performance, and strategies like choosing a random pivot are used to avoid the worst-case scenario.
